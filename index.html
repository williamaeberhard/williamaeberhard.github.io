About me

I obtained my PhD in Statistics in 2015 in a cotutelle between the University of Geneva and the University of Sydney. I then worked as a post-doctoral fellow at Dalhousie University as part of a Canadian Statistical Sciences Institute collaborative research team. Since 2018, I am an Assistant Professor of Statistics in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at Stevens Institute of Technology.

My research interests lie in robust statistics, semi-parametric methods, computational statistics, and spatio-temporal modeling with ecological applications. My current methodological research includes robust smoothing parameter selection for non-parametric additive models and automatic outlier detection for general state space models. Ongoing cross-disciplinary collaborations include growth estimation methods from mark-recapture data and large-scale spatio-temporal modeling of the impact of volcanic eruptions on vegetation density from satellite imagery.
